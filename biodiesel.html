Optimization of Diesel Engine Performance

Experimental study on optimizing operational parameters for improving the efficiency and reducing emissions of a diesel engine using biodiesel blends.

Organization: IIITDM Kurnool
Role: Researcher, Coauthor
Team Size: 2

Project Overview

The project investigates the use of biodiesel blends in diesel engines to enhance efficiency while minimizing harmful emissions. Experiments were conducted by varying key parameters such as fuel blend percentage, injection timing, and air-fuel ratio to determine optimal conditions.

Methodology

  • Experimental analysis on a single-cylinder, four-stroke diesel engine.
  • Utilized the Transesterification process to derive biodiesel from cottonseed oil.
  • Tested engine efficiency and emissions with different biodiesel blend ratios (D100, D95B5, etc.).
  • Applied Taguchi Optimization Technique to determine the best-performing parameters.

Key Findings

  • Efficiency increased by 2-3% with optimal biodiesel blend.
  • Significant reduction in harmful emissions (HC, CO, NOx) with biodiesel blends.
  • Optimal performance was achieved at a 20% biodiesel blend (D80B20)**.
