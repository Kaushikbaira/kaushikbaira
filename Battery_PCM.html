Thermal Management of Lithium-Ion Battery Pack Using PCM

Enhancing the thermal stability of lithium-ion batteries in electric vehicles through the integration of Phase Change Material (PCM) to regulate temperature fluctuations.

Organization: IIITDM Kurnool
Role: Lead Researcher
Team Size: 1

Project Overview

This project investigates the use of Phase Change Material (PCM) in lithium-ion battery packs to improve thermal management. Using ANSYS simulations, the study analyzes heat dissipation during charging and discharging cycles to prevent overheating and extend battery lifespan.

Key Aspects

  • Developed a **computational model** of the battery pack in ANSYS.
  • Selected **Eicosane** as the PCM due to its optimal phase transition temperature and high latent heat capacity.
  • Simulated **various operational conditions** to evaluate temperature regulation.
  • Achieved **a reduction in peak temperatures**, improving thermal stability and battery efficiency.

Findings & Impact

  • PCM effectively **stabilized battery temperature**, reducing overheating risks.

  • Optimized design enhances **battery safety, lifespan, and overall performance** in EV applications.
